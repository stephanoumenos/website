Switzerland spends [CHF 912 million a year](https://www.agrarbericht.ch/de/politik/direktzahlungen/finanzielle-mittel-fuer-direktzahlungen) on _Versorgungssicherheitsbeiträge_ — food supply security contributions — to its farmers. Net of imported animal feed, the country's [food self-sufficiency is 46%](https://2024.agrarbericht.ch/de/markt/marktentwicklungen/selbstversorgungsgrad).

The reason is land. Switzerland devotes 35% of its surface to agriculture. Of that agricultural land, [87% serves livestock](https://www.agrarbericht.ch/de/produktion/pflanzliche-produktion/flaechennutzung) — permanent grassland, alpine pastures, and feed crops. The remaining 13%, amounting to 4.4% of the country, produces food consumed directly by humans. The country accordingly produces [106% of its dairy needs and 96% of its beef](https://2024.agrarbericht.ch/de/markt/marktentwicklungen/selbstversorgungsgrad), but [49% of its vegetables, 49% of its cereals, and 23% of its plant oils](https://2024.agrarbericht.ch/de/markt/marktentwicklungen/selbstversorgungsgrad).

## Land use

A third is forest. The remainder is where [9 million people](https://www.bfs.admin.ch/asset/en/34447375) share space with [1.5 million cattle, 1.3 million pigs, and 13.3 million poultry](https://www.agrarbericht.ch/de/produktion/tierische-produktion/nutztierhalter-und-nutztierbestaende).

Animal agriculture — grassland, alpine pastures, and the [60% of arable land](https://www.agrarbericht.ch/de/produktion/pflanzliche-produktion/flaechennutzung) used for feed — occupies 12,736 km², roughly 12 times the [1,078 km²](https://www.are.admin.ch/de/bauzonen) zoned for residential use. Over [80%](https://www.are.admin.ch/de/bauzonen) of residential zones are already built on.

An obvious objection: 530,000 hectares of _Sömmerungsgebiet_ — alpine summer pastures — cannot grow crops regardless of policy. Grant the point entirely. Exclude them. What remains is 1,042,000 hectares of _landwirtschaftliche Nutzfläche_ in the lowlands and hills. Of this, 398,000 hectares are classified as arable — productive soil, concentrated in the Mittelland, capable of growing anything. Sixty per cent of it grows animal feed: [120,000 hectares of _Kunstwiesen_](https://www.eagff.ch/kunstwiesen-und-zwischenkulturen/kunstfutterbau-bedeutung/flaeche-der-kunstwiesen/flaeche-der-kunstwiesen) (temporary grass leys planted in crop rotation on arable-capable soil), [47,000 hectares of silage maize](https://www.schweizerbauer.ch/pflanzen/ackerbau/weniger-weizen-mehr-silomais), and roughly [60,000 hectares of feed grain](https://www.sbv-usp.ch/de/agristat-aktuell-08-25-schaetzung-der-schweizer-ackerflaeche-2025). These are not mountain meadows. They are lowland fields that could grow wheat, rapeseed, or legumes.

The government's own land classification underlines this. The [Sachplan Fruchtfolgeflächen](https://www.are.admin.ch/de/fff) protects [445,680 hectares](https://www.are.admin.ch/de/fff-statistik) of Switzerland's best soil — defined as land with slope under 18%, soil depth above 50 cm, and adequate climate — for the express purpose of guaranteeing food supply in severe shortages. Virtually all arable land qualifies. More than half of it currently produces animal feed. The government maintains a buffer of just 7,220 hectares above the federal minimum to protect this soil for _Versorgungssicherheit_, while the majority of what it protects feeds livestock.

## Food security

The _Versorgungssicherheitsbeiträge_ are the single largest category of Swiss direct payments to farmers. The objective is national self-sufficiency in a crisis. Federal data suggest the system undermines it: [gross self-sufficiency is 53%](https://2024.agrarbericht.ch/de/markt/marktentwicklungen/selbstversorgungsgrad), net of imported feed [46%](https://2024.agrarbericht.ch/de/markt/marktentwicklungen/selbstversorgungsgrad). The country overproduces dairy and beef while importing the majority of its grains, oils, and vegetables.

The modelling literature is consistent. A [2025 study by ETH Zurich and FiBL](https://www.srf.ch/news/dialog/lebensmittelproduktion-schweizer-landwirtschaft-koennte-die-gesamte-bevoelkerung-ernaehren) concluded that Switzerland could achieve full self-sufficiency without imports if arable land currently growing feed were converted to crops for human consumption. Agroscope, the federal government's own research institute, [modelled](https://www.agrarforschungschweiz.ch/en/2023/12/how-much-livestock-does-switzerland-need-for-optimal-land-use/) the optimal livestock population under the Climate Strategy 2050 and arrived at 42% fewer cattle and 65% fewer pigs. A [2024 paper in _Communications Earth & Environment_](https://www.nature.com/articles/s43247-023-01139-z) identified 181,479 hectares suitable for grain legume production — much of it currently under fodder maize — capable of replacing roughly 41% of Switzerland's animal protein.

## The subsidy structure

Average agricultural income per farm in 2024 was [CHF 81,700](https://www.agroscope.admin.ch/agroscope/en/home/news/newsroom/2025/11-18_landwirtschaftliches-einkommen-2024.html). Average direct payments per farm: CHF 79,000. For the typical Swiss farm, the subsidy approximates the income. Roughly [four-fifths](https://www.foraus.ch/en/posts/the-need-for-transparency-in-swiss-agriculture/) flows to animal production. The sector contributes [0.63% of GDP](https://www.eda.admin.ch/aboutswitzerland/en/home/wirtschaft/taetigkeitsgebiete/landwirtschaft.html) and employs [1.9% of the workforce](https://www.bfs.admin.ch/bfs/en/home/statistics/agriculture-forestry/farming.html) across 48,000 farms, half the number operating in 1996.

The federal government paid [CHF 2.788 billion](https://www.agrarbericht.ch/de/politik/direktzahlungen/finanzielle-mittel-fuer-direktzahlungen) in _Direktzahlungen_ in 2024. Parliament approved [CHF 14.2 billion](https://www.agrarbericht.ch/de/politik/uebersicht/bundesausgaben-fuer-landwirtschaft-und-ernaehrung) for 2026–2029 by a vote of [187 to 2](https://www.swissinfo.ch/eng/swiss-politics/national-wants-more-money-for-agriculture/88613983). But direct payments are only part of the transfer. The OECD's [2025 evaluation](https://www.oecd.org/en/publications/2025/10/agricultural-policy-monitoring-and-evaluation-2025_354e7040/full-report/switzerland_9badc265.html) estimates that **49%** of Swiss gross farm receipts derive from government support — budgetary payments and the price effects of trade barriers combined — the highest it has measured for any country in the period. The OECD average is 13%; the EU's, 19%. Only Norway and Iceland are comparable. New Zealand, with a similar pastoral sector, operates at 0.3%.

The trade barriers are not symmetric. Swiss tariffs on [dairy average 111%](https://www.wto.org/english/res_e/statis_e/daily_update_e/tariff_profiles/ch_e.pdf); on [meat, 95%](https://www.wto.org/english/res_e/statis_e/daily_update_e/tariff_profiles/ch_e.pdf). On cereals: 16%. Fruits and vegetables: 17%. Oilseeds: 20%. Animal products are shielded at five to seven times the rate of plant products — sustaining a production structure the government's own research says should contract. The result: domestic producer prices [47% above world reference levels](https://www.oecd.org/en/publications/2025/10/agricultural-policy-monitoring-and-evaluation-2025_354e7040/full-report/switzerland_9badc265.html) and consumer food prices [roughly 60% above the EU average](https://ec.europa.eu/eurostat/statistics-explained/index.php?title=Comparative_price_levels_for_food,_beverages_and_tobacco).

Avenir Suisse's [full accounting](https://www.avenir-suisse.ch/en/publication/swiss-agriculture-continues-to-enjoy-growing-economic-privileges/) — direct subsidies, trade protection, environmental externalities, foregone export revenue — arrives at **CHF 20.7 billion per year**, or [CHF 2,300 per household](https://www.avenir-suisse.ch/en/publication/swiss-agriculture-continues-to-enjoy-growing-economic-privileges/).

## Environmental damage

Swiss agriculture accounts for [92% of national ammonia emissions](https://www.agrarforschungschweiz.ch/en/2022/11/ammonia-emissions-from-agriculture-in-switzerland-for-1990-to-2020/). [93%](https://www.agrarforschungschweiz.ch/en/2022/11/ammonia-emissions-from-agriculture-in-switzerland-for-1990-to-2020/) of agricultural ammonia comes from livestock; cattle alone contribute 77%. Despite decades of reduction targets, emissions have been [effectively stagnant since 2004](https://www.agrarforschungschweiz.ch/en/2022/11/ammonia-emissions-from-agriculture-in-switzerland-for-1990-to-2020/). Nitrogen [critical loads are exceeded](https://www.bafu.admin.ch/bafu/en/home/topics/air/info-specialists/air-quality-in-switzerland/nitrogen-containing-air-pollutants-affect-biodiversity.html) across large parts of the country, degrading forests, wetlands, and alpine grasslands. [70,000 tonnes of nitrogen](https://www.bafu.admin.ch/bafu/en/home/topics/chemicals/publications-studies/publications/nitrogen-flows-switzerland-2020.html) entered Swiss water bodies in 2020; the federal target to halve agricultural nitrogen inputs relative to 1985 has not been met. Over [70%](https://www.foraus.ch/en/posts/the-need-for-transparency-in-swiss-agriculture/) of agricultural subsidies are assessed as potentially harmful to biodiversity.

Agriculture also contributes [16% of Swiss greenhouse gas emissions](https://www.bafu.admin.ch/bafu/en/home/topics/climate/data/greenhouse-gas-inventory/agriculture.html), predominantly methane and nitrous oxide from ruminants. The government's own [Climate Strategy for Agriculture and Food 2050](https://www.swissinfo.ch/eng/science/the-swiss-have-to-eat-less-meat-by-2050-heres-how/80558069) states that meat consumption is "still too high" and targets a two-thirds reduction in per-person food-related emissions by 2050. Simultaneously, the federal government spends approximately [CHF 39 million per year](https://www.greenpeace.ch/de/story/63645/) on public promotion of animal products — meat, dairy, cheese, eggs — against [CHF 8 million](https://www.greenpeace.ch/de/story/63645/) for plant-based foods. The [Federal Audit Office recommended](https://www.swissinfo.ch/eng/agribusiness/dutch-cities-are-banning-meat-advertising-will-switzerland-follow/91078093) discontinuing meat promotion funding in 2018. The recommendation was not followed.

## Housing

The protected agricultural land and the housing shortage occupy the same geography. The Fruchtfolgeflächen are concentrated in the Mittelland — flat, accessible, well-connected — which is also where the majority of Switzerland's population lives and housing demand is highest. The [ARE](https://www.are.admin.ch/de/fff) notes the tension explicitly: these soils are well suited for settlement development by virtue of the same topography and location that makes them prime farmland.

The [Raumplanungsgesetz](https://www.fedlex.admin.ch/eli/cc/1979/1573_1573_1573/de) (RPG), [revised in 2013](https://www.are.admin.ch/de/rpg1), caps building zones at 15 years of projected demand. When agricultural land is rezoned for construction, a [_Mehrwertabgabe_](https://www.are.admin.ch/de/rpg1) of at least 20% is levied. When Fruchtfolgeflächen are consumed, several cantons require full compensation with equivalent-quality soil elsewhere — and equivalent-quality soil is scarce. Since the revision, [173 municipalities](https://www.schweizerbauer.ch/artikel/390-ha-bauland-zurueck-in-landwirtschaftszone) have rezoned 390 hectares of building land back to agricultural zones, while the vacancy rate was falling to its lowest level in a decade. Between 2009 and 2018, [90%](https://www.bfs.admin.ch/asset/de/19365051) of new settlement area came from former farmland — a source the RPG now restricts.

Housing is confined to [2,343 km² of building zones](https://www.are.admin.ch/de/bauzonen), over 80% already built. Switzerland's long-run housing supply elasticity — the responsiveness of new construction to price signals — is [0.15](https://www.oecd.org/en/publications/the-price-responsiveness-of-housing-supply-in-oecd-countries_5kgk9qhrnn33-en.html), among the lowest in the OECD: roughly a third of France or Germany's, a tenth of the United States'. The federal government [estimates](https://www.bwo.admin.ch/de/nsb?id=104311) annual housing demand at approximately 50,000 dwellings; around 40,000 were completed in 2024. Building permits have [declined more than 30%](https://www.bwo.admin.ch/de/nsb?id=104311) since 2016. The national vacancy rate has fallen to [1.00%](https://www.bfs.admin.ch/asset/en/36153137). In Zurich it is [0.10%](https://www.stadt-zuerich.ch/de/aktuell/medienmitteilungen/2025/08/leerwohnungsziffer-bleibt-mit-01-prozent-auf-sehr-tiefem-niveau.html). Asking rents have risen [4.7%, 3.2%, and 2.3%](https://swissmarketplace.group/media-release/mietindex-12-2025-en) in three consecutive years.

The RPG prescribes _Siedlungsentwicklung nach innen_ — inward densification — as the alternative to outward expansion. A [2024 study by the Hochschule Luzern](https://hub.hslu.ch/immobilienblog/2024/02/07/warum-unueberbaute-bauzonen-das-bevoelkerungswachstum-nicht-bewaeltigen-koennen/) found that only 4,737 hectares of undeveloped residential zones are in well-serviced locations, against projected population growth of 18.4% through 2050. The gap is structural, and the densification mandate, on current evidence, cannot close it.

The [Swiss constitution](https://www.fedlex.admin.ch/eli/cc/1999/404/en) mandates both agricultural food security (Article 104) and the promotion of housing (Article 108). Voters have been asked about agricultural reform repeatedly — the [_Massentierhaltungsinitiative_](https://www.admin.ch/gov/en/start/documentation/votes/20220925/factory-farming-initiative.html) in 2022, the [_Trinkwasserinitiative_](https://www.admin.ch/gov/en/start/documentation/votes/20210613/popular-initiative-for-clean-drinking-water-and-healthy-food.html) in 2021, the [_Biodiversitätsinitiative_](https://www.admin.ch/gov/en/start/documentation/votes/20240922/biodiversity-initiative.html) in 2024 — and rejected each one by margins above 60%. The [Bilaterals III package](https://www.europa.eda.admin.ch/en/package-switzerland-eu) signed in March 2026 — the most consequential renegotiation of Swiss-EU relations in a generation — explicitly preserved the entire agricultural tariff regime and excluded agriculture from the dynamic EU law alignment that applies to every other updated agreement. None of these processes directly addressed whether the land allocation achieves the food security it invokes.

## Appendix: data sources and methodology

### Land use

The land use figures are derived from two datasets:

1. **[Arealstatistik 2013/18](https://www.bfs.admin.ch/bfs/de/home/statistiken/raum-umwelt/bodennutzung-bedeckung/landwirtschaftsflaechen.html)** — BFS land use survey from aerial photographs. Provides the top-level breakdown: agriculture (35.2%), forest (31.3%), settlement (7.9%), unproductive (25.6%), and the within-agriculture split into arable (30.9%), permanent grassland (34.4%), and alpine pastures (34.7%).
2. **[Agrarbericht 2025](https://www.agrarbericht.ch/de/produktion/pflanzliche-produktion/flaechennutzung)** — BLW agricultural report. Used for the feed/food split of arable land. The 60% feed share counts _Kunstwiesen_ (temporary grass leys on arable-capable land), silage maize, and feed grain. A narrower definition excluding Kunstwiesen yields roughly 43%.

The livestock land figure (12,736 km²) is calculated as: all permanent grassland + all alpine pastures + 60% of arable land. The 530,000 hectares of _Sömmerungsgebiet_ (alpine summer pastures) cannot grow crops, but even excluding them entirely, 81% of the remaining 1,042,000 hectares of _landwirtschaftliche Nutzfläche_ serves livestock. The arable feed breakdown: ~120,000 ha _Kunstwiesen_ ([eAGFF](https://www.eagff.ch/kunstwiesen-und-zwischenkulturen/kunstfutterbau-bedeutung/flaeche-der-kunstwiesen/flaeche-der-kunstwiesen)), ~47,000 ha silage maize ([Schweizer Bauer](https://www.schweizerbauer.ch/pflanzen/ackerbau/weniger-weizen-mehr-silomais)), ~60,000 ha feed grain ([SBV Agristat](https://www.sbv-usp.ch/de/agristat-aktuell-08-25-schaetzung-der-schweizer-ackerflaeche-2025)). All are lowland crops on Mittelland soil.

The residential figure (1,078 km²) comes from the **[ARE Bauzonenstatistik 2022](https://www.are.admin.ch/de/bauzonen)**: 234,337 hectares of total building zones, of which 46% are _Wohnzonen_.

The interactive map combines **[Landwirtschaftliche Nutzungsflächen](https://www.geodienste.ch/services/lwb_nutzungsflaechen)** (agricultural parcels, geodienste.ch) and **[Bauzonen Schweiz](https://opendata.swiss/en/dataset/bauzonen-schweiz-harmonisiert)** (harmonised building zones, KGK-CGC), reprojected to WGS84.

- Permanent grassland (~604,000 ha) + 60% of arable land (~237,000 ha)

§3. THE SPATIAL PLANNING BOTTLENECK — PRIMARY SOURCES
========================================================================

RAUMPLANUNGSGESETZ (RPG) REVISION 2014
- RPG 1: approved by popular vote March 3, 2013; entered force May 1, 2014.
- Art. 15 RPG: building zones must correspond to 15-year anticipated need.
  Oversized zones must be reduced. Cantons had 5 years to adapt Richtpläne.
  Construction zone moratorium during adaptation period.
- Mehrwertabgabe (value capture levy): ≥20% when ag land rezoned to building land.
- Siedlungsentwicklung nach innen (inward development) mandated over sprawl.
- All cantons adapted their Richtpläne by October 2022.
  https://www.are.admin.ch/de/rpg1
  Law text: https://www.fedlex.admin.ch/eli/cc/1979/1573_1573_1573/de

REZONING BACK TO AGRICULTURE (AUSZONUNG)
- 173 municipalities have converted 390 hectares of building land back to
  agricultural zones — while vacancy rates are at their lowest since 2013.
  https://www.schweizerbauer.ch/artikel/390-ha-bauland-zurueck-in-landwirtschaftszone
  Context: https://www.uvek.admin.ch/dam/de/sd-web/W7yPWa5s4UAL/bauzonen.pdf

FRUCHTFOLGEFLÄCHEN (CROP ROTATION AREAS)
- 445,680 ha of FFF currently secured (~11% of total land area).
- Federal minimum requirement: 438,460 ha. Surplus: only 7,220 ha.
- Revised Sachplan FFF approved by Federal Council May 8, 2020.
- Purpose: ensure food security during severe supply crises.
- FFF are under pressure from settlement expansion, energy infrastructure, recreation.
  https://www.are.admin.ch/de/fff
  https://www.are.admin.ch/de/fff-statistik
  Sachplan PDF: https://www.are.admin.ch/dam/are/de/dokumente/sachplan_fruchtfolgeflaechenfff.pdf

RPG 2 (SECOND REVISION)
- Parliament passed RPG 2 on September 29, 2023 (indirect counterproposal to

  https://www.are.admin.ch/de/rpg2

AMMONIA EMISSIONS
- Agriculture: 92% of total Swiss ammonia. Livestock: 93% of agricultural ammonia.
- Cattle: 77% of livestock ammonia. Pigs: declined 49% since 1990. Poultry: +32%.
- Farm-manure spreading: 44%. Housing/outdoor yards: 36%.
- Total decline: 23% since 1990, but stagnant since ~2000.
- Critical load thresholds exceeded in large parts of Switzerland — eutrophication
  and acidification of forests, bogs, species-rich grasslands, alpine heathland.
  https://www.agrarforschungschweiz.ch/en/2022/11/ammonia-emissions-from-agriculture-in-switzerland-for-1990-to-2020/
  https://www.bafu.admin.ch/bafu/en/home/topics/air/info-specialists/air-quality-in-switzerland/nitrogen-containing-air-pollutants-affect-biodiversity.html

GREENHOUSE GAS EMISSIONS
- Agriculture: 16% of Swiss GHG emissions (mainly CH₄ and N₂O).
- 56% enteric fermentation (ruminants), 25% agricultural soils, 18% manure management.
  https://www.bafu.admin.ch/bafu/en/home/topics/climate/data/greenhouse-gas-inventory/agriculture.html
  https://www.agroscope.admin.ch/agroscope/en/home/topics/environment-resources/climate-air-quality/treibhausgas-emissionen/swiss-national-greenhouse-gas-inventory-agriculture.html

NITROGEN FLOWS
- ~70,000 tonnes of nitrogen entered Swiss water bodies in 2020.
- Agriculture target to reduce N inputs to water by 50% vs 1985 was not met (gap: ~8,000 t).
- Nitrogen surpluses stagnating since late 1990s, particularly high in regions with
  excessive livestock density and imported feed.
  https://www.bafu.admin.ch/bafu/en/home/topics/chemicals/publications-studies/publications/nitrogen-flows-switzerland-2020.html
  Full PDF: https://www.bafu.admin.ch/dam/bafu/en/dokumente/chemikalien/uw-umwelt-wissen/stickstofffluesseinderschweiz2020stoffflussanalyseundentwicklung.pdf

- Swiss agriculture could feed the entire population without imports if land currently

- Dairy cows: 545,485 (vs ~676,000 → 19% reduction).

- Current grain legume production: ~9,000 ha (negligible).
- Scenario I: 107,734 ha suitable on existing arable land.

  https://www.nature.com/articles/s43247-023-01139-z

- ~70% of Swiss agricultural land is grassland used for livestock feed.

  https://www.frontiers.org/journals/sustainable-food-systems/articles/10.3389/fsufs.2025.1548480/full

SWISS CONSTITUTION (Fedlex, English)
- Article 75: Confederation lays down principles on spatial planning for appropriate
  and economic use of land and properly ordered settlement.
- Article 75b: Second homes capped at max 20% per municipality (approved March 11, 2012).
- Article 104: Agriculture must contribute to reliable food provision, conservation of
  natural resources, upkeep of countryside, and decentralized settlement.
  Multi-functional duties.
- Article 108: Confederation promotes housing construction, acquisition of property,
  and non-profit housing. Promotes land acquisition for housing, cost reduction.
  https://www.fedlex.admin.ch/eli/cc/1999/404/en

The tension: Art. 104 protects agriculture's multi-functional role and Art. 108 mandates
housing promotion — but Art. 104 does not specify that agriculture must be livestock-based,
and Art. 108's housing mandate is being undermined by the land allocation to livestock.
